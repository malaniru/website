1. AI Market Trends

Anthropic × Gates Foundation — $200M, four years, May 14. Anthropic and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation announced a four-year, $200M partnership combining grant funding, Claude usage credits, and technical support across four named programs: global health, life sciences, education, and economic mobility. Named disease targets in the global health portion include polio, HPV, and eclampsia/preeclampsia. The education work names K–12 evidence-based tutoring in the US, career-transition guidance, and foundational literacy and numeracy apps for sub-Saharan Africa and India. The economic-mobility leg targets smallholder-farming productivity for the roughly two billion people whose incomes depend on it, including agriculture-specific Claude improvements, local-crop datasets, and agriculture-domain evaluation benchmarks. The $200M figure is the announced total commitment; the breakdown between cash grants, Claude credits, and engineering support is not itemized in the public materials.

Anthropic × PwC alliance expansion — May 14. PwC announced an expansion of its multi-year strategic alliance with Anthropic: Claude Code and Claude Cowork rolling out across PwC's professional workforce starting in the US and expanding globally, and a new standalone PwC business unit, Office of the CFO, built around Claude as its first AI-anchored business line. PwC reports Claude already in production across professional-sports operations, insurance underwriting, mainframe modernization, HR transformation, and cybersecurity engagements, with delivery-time reductions of up to 70% (partner-reported, not independently audited).

Anthropic billing restructure — May 14, effective June 15. Beginning June 15, four surfaces will move out of the chat-subscription token pool into a separate, API-style credit pool: the Claude Agent SDK (Python and TypeScript), claude -p headless mode, the official Claude Code GitHub Actions, and any third-party application built on the Agent SDK. Credit allocations follow the subscription tier: $20/month (Pro), $100/month (Max 5x), $200/month (Max 20x). Standard interactive surfaces (web, desktop, mobile, Claude Code in terminal, Claude Cowork) remain inside the existing subscription pool. Anthropic's stated rationale: super-linear demand growth since the start of 2026, with Sonnet 4.6 and Opus 4.7 launches further tightening inference compute.

OpenAI shipped Codex on mobile (May 14) and ChatGPT for personal finance (May 15). Codex came to the ChatGPT mobile app in preview, giving users a way to review work, approve commands, steer threads, and stay connected to active coding agents from the phone. Personal-finance tools launched in preview for US ChatGPT Pro subscribers — connect bank, brokerage, and credit-card accounts via Plaid across 12,000+ institutions including Schwab, Fidelity, Chase, Robinhood, American Express, and Capital One.

Anthropic $50B raise at $900B — round expected to close end of May.
