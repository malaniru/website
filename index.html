A Malaniru maturity model

The AI Maturity Scale

A shared language for where an organization stands today and where the engagement takes it. This is a Malaniru maturity model for framing an engagement — not an industry standard, accreditation, or certification. Select any level to read what it looks like in practice.

Most clients start here · Levels 1–2 Where the engagement takes you · Levels 3–5
1 Unaware / Ad Hoc Details

No written policy. AI use is uncontrolled, invisible, or banned by default. Decisions are made case by case with no record.

2 Reactive / Foundational Details

Some informal rules exist. Problems are addressed after they occur. Awareness is starting but inconsistent.

3 Defined / Governed Details

Documented policy, clear ownership, consistent rules applied across the organization. People know what is and isn’t allowed.

4 Managed / Proactive Details

Governance is monitored and reviewed on a cadence. Risk is anticipated, not just handled. Staff are trained. Incidents feed back into the policy.

5 Optimized / Enabling Details

Policy actively accelerates safe adoption. Continuous improvement loop, metrics, embedded AI fluency. Governance is an asset, not a brake.

Most organizations arrive at Level 1–2. The engagement is designed to reach Level 3, extending into Levels 4–5 with measurement and training.

The four-step path

How an organization climbs the scale

The adoption journey in four sequential steps. The order matters: governance before usage, usage before workflow integration, integration before durable documentation.

  1. → 1

    Policies

    Put the governance framework in place first — the rules that define which tools are approved, what data may and may not go into them, who reviews AI output before it leaves the organization, and which decisions are never delegated to AI. Policies come first because you cannot safely enable AI use you haven’t bounded; every ungoverned use is an unmanaged risk.

    See the Policies Summary →
  2. → 2

    Responsible Use

    Build foundational AI awareness and the verification habits that make AI use trustworthy: understanding what the tools do and don’t do, recognizing failure modes, and treating a wrong answer as worse than no answer. Staff move from being aware that AI carries risk to being equipped to use it deliberately.

    Explore AI Fluency →
  3. → 3

    Workflow Integration

    Hands-on, workflow-specific training inside the team’s actual daily operations — not generic demos. The team learns to use AI within the real tasks they already do, in a trimmed-down Claude Cowork environment configured for their work. This is the step that converts awareness into capability.

    Explore the Workspace →
  4. 4

    Thoughtful Use (Building SOPs)

    Turn the team’s personal, in-their-heads knowledge into documented, repeatable standard operating procedures. This is what makes the gains durable: the organization keeps the capability when people are busy, when staff change, and when the founder isn’t in the room.
