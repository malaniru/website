Released June 30 and announced July 1, Sonnet 5 is described by Anthropic as its most agentic Sonnet yet, with performance "close to Opus 4.8" at materially lower prices — introductory pricing of $2 per million input tokens and $10 per million output tokens through August 31, then $3/$15 — a 1M-token context window, 128K max output, and adaptive thinking on by default; it is now the default model for Free and Pro and is available in Claude Code, the Claude Platform, and the API (Source: Anthropic, "Introducing Claude Sonnet 5"; Claude Platform docs; Simon Willison, June 30, 2026). 1. AI Market Trends

This week the deployable tier got stronger, and the frontier tier got a countdown.

First, and dominant for delivery work: Claude Sonnet 5 shipped. Anthropic released Sonnet 5 on June 30 (announced July 1) as "the most agentic Sonnet model yet," able to plan, use tools like browsers and terminals, and run autonomously at a level that "just a few months ago required larger and more expensive models," with performance the company positions as close to Opus 4.8 (Source: Anthropic, "Introducing Claude Sonnet 5" — vendor-stated; benchmark parity self-reported). It carries a 1,000,000-token context window and 128,000 max output tokens, adaptive thinking on by default (manual extended thinking now returns a 400 error, as it did on Sonnet 4.6), and it is the default model for Free and Pro while also available to Max, Team, and Enterprise, in Claude Code and via the API as claude-sonnet-5 (Source: Claude Platform docs, "What's new in Claude Sonnet 5"; Simon Willison, June 30). Introductory pricing is $2/M input and $10/M output through August 31, 2026, then $3/M and $15/M (Source: Anthropic; IT-Connect, 2026). The strategic read: this lands the same week Anthropic is preparing to go public, and it strengthens exactly the tier most teams build on — a governable, generally available model with near-flagship capability at a workhorse price.

Second: Fable 5's clock is ticking toward July 7. Restored July 1 under a government-coordinated safety classifier, Fable 5 is included for up to 50% of weekly usage limits through July 7; after that it exits subscription limits and moves to usage credits. Anthropic frames this as capacity-driven and temporary, saying Fable is not permanently leaving subscriptions and will be restored "as soon as capacity allows," and that its improved classifier blocks the specific reported technique in over 99% of cases (Source: Anthropic, "Redeploying Claude Fable 5"; BleepingComputer; MarkTechPost, July 1). Mythos 5, the tightly controlled cyber model, remains administered to roughly 100-plus US critical-infrastructure organizations (Source: 9to5Mac, June 26).

Third: the Google talent story keeps its shadow over Gemini. No new named departure has been confirmed since Bloomberg's June 24 report that Gemini researchers Jonas Adler and Alexander Pritzel are leaving for Anthropic (following Nobel laureate John Jumper to Anthropic and Noam Shazeer to OpenAI), but the episode still frames Gemini 3.5 Pro's continued slip. Jumper's exit alone reportedly coincided with a single-session Alphabet decline variously reported at roughly $225B–$245B in market value (Source: Bloomberg, June 24; Inc., 2026 — the dollar figure and its causal link are reported, not proven). Treat the market-cap figure as an attributed same-day move, not established cause and effect.
