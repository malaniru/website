1. AI Market Trends

Q1 2026: $300B in global venture funding. Crunchbase confirmed Q1 2026 set an all-time record, with investors deploying $300 billion across approximately 6,000 startups globally — up over 150% year-over-year. AI companies absorbed $242 billion, or 80% of total funding. Four of the five largest venture rounds in history closed in Q1: OpenAI ($122B), Anthropic ($30B), xAI ($20B), and Waymo ($16B) — collectively representing $188 billion, or 65% of all global venture capital in the quarter. (Source: Crunchbase, "Q1 2026 Shatters Venture Funding Records," April 2026)

Foundational AI funding doubled all of 2025 in one quarter. Crunchbase's sector snapshot notes that venture funding to foundational AI startups in Q1 2026 was double the full-year 2025 total, with OpenAI, Anthropic, and xAI accounting for the majority. (Source: Crunchbase, "Sector Snapshot: Venture Funding To Foundational AI Startups," April 2026)

Enterprise AI adoption hits 88%, but spending consolidates. Survey data shows 88% of companies now report regular AI use in at least one business function, up from 78% in the prior period. VCs predict enterprises will increase AI budgets in 2026 while concentrating spending through fewer vendors. (Source: TechCrunch, December 2025)

Agentic AI sector: $2.66B raised across 44 rounds in 2026. A 142.6% rise in agentic AI funding compared to 2025, with Replit ($922M total, $9B valuation) as the highest-funded company in the sector. Reflection AI is in talks for a $2.5B round at $25B valuation. (Source: Tracxn; IndexBox; The AI Insider)

What This Means for You. When 65% of all global VC flows into four companies, the downstream effect is vendor lock-in at scale. For organizations adopting AI, the enterprise consolidation trend makes deliberate multi-vendor evaluation worthwhile — concentrate spending where it makes sense, but understand the dependency you are taking on. The 88% adoption figure means the question for most organizations has shifted from "should we adopt AI" to "how do we govern what we have already deployed."
