A continuity correction on suspension status. Recent reporting tracked Fable 5 and Mythos 5 as continuously offline since June 12. Multiple June 23 sources now state Fable 5 was offline only June 12 to around June 18 and has since returned, while at least one source still describes the models as suspended. This is a genuine source conflict. Best current reading: the export-control suspension and today's planned billing change are two separate events, and Fable 5 is available but now metered, while Mythos 5 appears to remain restricted to vetted partners. Verify exact availability via Anthropic's status page before any client-facing statement.

What This Means for You. For governance, today is a clean illustration of operating risk: a flagship model can be export-controlled by government action and a live outage can take the whole surface down in the same fortnight — the exact single-source-dependency exposure a readiness review should surface. For implementation policy, the multi-provider fallback shift validates governance frameworks that assume model substitution, not permanence. The practical guidance is direct: keep production work on Opus 4.8 / Sonnet 4.6 — stable, available, and half the cost of credit-metered Fable 5 — and treat frontier-tier models as evaluation targets, not load-bearing infrastructure.
