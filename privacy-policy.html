2. What we collect and why

Booking a discovery call

Our "Book a discovery call" links open Calendly. When you schedule a call, Calendly collects the information you provide — typically your name, email address, and any details you add — and shares your booking with us so we can meet. This data is processed to arrange and manage the meeting you request.

Intake and questionnaire forms

If you complete one of our intake or questionnaire forms (for example, the AI-readiness, operational, or security & compliance forms), the answers you submit are sent to a Google Apps Script endpoint and stored in a Google Sheet that we control. We use this information to understand your organization's needs and respond to your enquiry.

Needs legal review Confirm exactly which fields each form collects, whether any special-category data (GDPR Art. 9) could be entered, and that the Google Sheet is access-restricted. Confirm a Data Processing Agreement is in place with Google.

Information collected automatically

  • Hosting logs. This site is served via GitHub Pages. GitHub may automatically log technical data such as your IP address and browser type to deliver the site and protect against abuse.
  • Fonts. Pages load the Inter typeface from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com / fonts.gstatic.com). Your browser's request to Google may expose your IP address to Google.
Needs legal review Google Fonts loaded from Google's servers has been challenged under the GDPR (e.g. the 2022 Munich ruling). Consider self-hosting the font files to avoid transmitting visitor IP addresses to Google. Confirm whether any first-party or third-party cookies are set; a static site typically sets none, but embedded third parties may.
