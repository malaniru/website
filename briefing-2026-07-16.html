1. AI Market Trends

Wednesday's tape split cleanly in two: a hard memory-chip repricing on top of a resilient broad market. The driver this time was competitive, not macro — a China DRAM supply story landing directly on the names the AI trade had bid up.

First, the CXMT catalyst. China's ChangXin Memory Technologies set the terms of a roughly $8.5 billion IPO (about 57.9 billion yuan before any over-allotment) on Shanghai's STAR Market, with a July 27 listing — Asia's largest IPO of 2026 to date and the biggest Chinese A-share semiconductor offering on record (Source: MarketScreener, July 2026; Digitimes, "CXMT's US$8.5 billion IPO signals China's accelerating challenge to global memory leaders," July 16 — press). CXMT is the world's fourth-largest DRAM maker, with a share that reportedly tripled year over year to roughly 7.7-8% (Source: Bloomberg, July 9; Modern Diplomacy, "What Is CXMT and Why Is It China's Leading DRAM Chipmaker," July 15 — press; market-share figures directional, re-confirm before external use). The market read a well-funded domestic Chinese DRAM challenger as a structural risk to memory pricing power, and repriced the incumbents accordingly.

Second, the memory selloff was sharp and concentrated. Micron fell about 8-9% on the session — a handful of outlets put the intraday move nearer 10% — dragging AMD, Intel, and Marvell each down more than 5% and SanDisk more than 11% (Source: 24/7 Wall St., July 15; Yahoo Finance, "Micron Drops 8% on China Competition Fears," July 15 — press). Note the whiplash: the same Micron that drew a KeyBanc target lift to $1,750 on tight supply on Tuesday sold off Wednesday on the mirror-image fear — that Chinese supply is about to loosen. Both narratives cannot be right for long; the honest read is that memory pricing power is now the contested variable, and one IPO filing was enough to flip the sign.

Third — and the reason the day was a rotation rather than a rout — the broad market absorbed the chip weakness and rose. The S&P 500 gained 0.38% to close at 7,572.40 and the Nasdaq Composite added 0.62% to 26,269.23, as investors moved out of high-flying semiconductors and into large-cap tech: Apple rose about 4% to a fresh all-time high, and Amazon, Alphabet, and Microsoft each gained roughly 3% (Source: CNBC, "Stock market news for July 15, 2026"; TheStreet, July 15 — press; intraday/close figures vary by feed). This is a healthier tape than a straight AI-wide drawdown: capital is discriminating within the AI complex, not fleeing it. Separately, TheStreet flagged that SpaceX's tracking stock dipped below its IPO price for the first time — a reminder that the 2026 listing wave is now trading in both directions (Source: TheStreet, July 15 — press; single-outlet, treat as reported).

Fourth, agentic-AI venture funding stayed heavy and moved up-market. July's agent-startup total is tracked near $1.8 billion across 12-plus deals, and the largest rounds this month skew toward enterprise search, developer platforms, and governed workflows: Harvey AI's reported $200 million Series C at a ~$2.1 billion valuation, Lovable's $200 million Series B at ~$2.8 billion, Glean's $180 million Series D at ~$2.7 billion, Hebbia's $130 million Series B at ~$1.0 billion, and — carried from yesterday — Norm AI's $120 million Series C for agentic compliance (Source: gravity.fast, "AI Agent Startup Funding Tracker: Q3 2026"; aifunding.me, "AI Agent Funding July 2026"; New Market Pitch; Crescendo.ai — press; private valuations unaudited, re-confirm before external use). The throughline is unchanged from prior briefings: capital concentrates in agent execution infrastructure, enterprise control planes, and regulated vertical workflows.

Three variants: Sol (flagship, with an "ultra" mode that delegates to submodels), Terra (mid-tier), Luna (low-cost). OpenAI's Sam Altman said Sol is 54% more token-efficient on agentic coding tasks; pricing runs Sol $5/$30, Terra $2.50/$15, Luna $1/$6 per million tokens. Alongside the models, OpenAI shipped ChatGPT Work, a redesigned desktop app with local file and browser access, Hosted Sites for publishing interactive pages, and a new GPT-Live voice family that can listen and speak simultaneously; Codex added new MCP approval and auth controls (writes app-approval mode, interactive MCP authentication, hosted login redirects) (Source: TechCrunch, July 9; Axios, July 9; OpenAI, "GPT-5.6," July 2026 — press/primary; efficiency figure vendor-reported).

MCP 2026-07-28 — release candidate, beta SDKs available (carry). The largest revision since launch is final on July 28 (now ~12 days out). The headline change is a stateless protocol core — sessions and the initialization handshake are removed, and the Streamable HTTP transport now requires Mcp-Method and Mcp-Name headers (SEP-2243) so gateways can route on the operation without inspecting the body. List and resource-read results carry ttlMs and cacheScope (SEP-2549). Roots, Sampling, and Logging are deprecated (SEP-2577) but, under a new lifecycle policy (SEP-2596), must stay deprecated at least twelve months before removal. An Extensions framework and MCP Apps (SEP-1865) round out the change. Beta SDKs for Python, TypeScript, Go, and C# are published against the RC (Source: Model Context Protocol blog, "The 2026-07-28 MCP Specification Release Candidate" and "Beta SDKs for the 2026-07-28 Spec" — primary).

Claude Code v2.1.210 (carry). Latest confirmed release, shipped July 15: transcript-tamper auto-mode rule, /config key=value syntax, sandbox.allowAppleEvents opt-in, --json-schema invalid-schema fix, Windows worktree-removal fix, and improved auto-retry on connection drops. No confirmed newer ship overnight (Source: Claude Code changelog; Releasebot, "Claude Code Updates — July 2026" — release-note aggregation; confirm against the official changelog).

Carry items (scheduled or unchanged): Fable 5 — included on paid plans through July 19 at up to 50% of weekly usage limits; prepaid usage credits ($10/M input, $50/M output) begin July 20. ChatGPT Work — OpenAI's Cowork-style agentic app, live since July 9. Anthropic IPO — confidential S-1 filed June 1 (last private round ~$965B valuation on a $65B Series H), targeting a fall listing.
