1. AI Market Trends

Microsoft FY26 Q3 earnings — print tonight (after market close). Sell-side consensus across previewing outlets converges on ~$81.4B revenue (+16.2% YoY), ~$4.06 adjusted EPS, Azure +37–38% constant currency, and quarterly capex ~$35.2B (Source: tradingkey.com, "Microsoft 2026 Q3 Earnings Preview," April 27, 2026; Alphastreet, "Microsoft's Q3 FY2026 Earnings Test," April 2026; coindcx.com, April 2026; geekwire.com, April 2026). Full-year FY26 capex guidance is reportedly tracking $110B–$120B, a step-up from approximately $80B in FY25 (Source: 247wallst.com, "Microsoft Falls 4% as $110 Billion AI Bet…," April 23, 2026). The two prints that matter for client conversations are (a) Azure deceleration vs. re-acceleration — Q2 reported was 39%, Q3 management guidance was 37–38% — and (b) whether quarterly capex prints at or above the $35B mark and what that implies for full-year capex. This is a same-day pending event. Any setup and training language describing Microsoft's cloud-substrate trajectory should be frozen until the print lands and then updated Thursday morning with the actuals.

Musk v. Altman — Day 3, Musk back on the stand. Per CNBC's live update, Musk retakes the stand Wednesday for a third day after a Tuesday session in which he (a) framed OpenAI's founding as a deliberate counterweight to Google after a personal disagreement with Larry Page over AI safety, (b) said he was "open to the idea of establishing a small for-profit subsidiary" but rejects the current structure in which the for-profit "takes the vast majority of the value away from the charity," and (c) reiterated his prediction that AI will be "smarter than any human as soon as next year" (Source: CNBC, "Musk v. Altman live updates: Musk to retake stand in day 3 of OpenAI trial," April 29, 2026; CNBC, "OpenAI trial day 2 takeaways," April 28, 2026; NPR, "Elon Musk testifies against OpenAI…," April 28, 2026; Newsweek, April 2026). Upcoming witnesses reportedly include Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella, and several early OpenAI researchers and engineers (Source: CNN Business, April 28, 2026). Continue treating trial outcomes as information surface, not betting market. The Nadella deposition, in particular, is a watch-item — anything Microsoft's CEO says under oath about OpenAI's structure or the partnership economics is an exceptionally clean compliance-teaching artifact regardless of verdict.

Open-multimodal frontier moves — NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Nano Omni. NVIDIA released Nemotron 3 Nano Omni on April 28, 2026 through Hugging Face, OpenRouter, build.nvidia.com, and 25+ partner platforms — a 30-billion-parameter model that activates only 3 billion parameters per token via a mixture-of-experts architecture, runs on a single GPU at ~25GB of RAM, supports a 256K context window, and unifies reasoning across video, audio, images, documents, and text (Source: NVIDIA Blog, April 28, 2026; NVIDIA Developer Blog, April 2026; Hugging Face, April 2026; OpenRouter Nemotron 3 Nano 30B A3B model card). Vendor-claimed throughput improvements: up to 9x higher vs. other open omni models on video and multi-document workloads, 7.4x system efficiency on multi-document, 9.2x system efficiency on video, and "topping six leaderboards" for document/video/audio understanding (Source: NVIDIA Blog, April 28, 2026; buildfastwithai.com, April 2026). Self-reported — independent third-party verification not yet available. Named adopters: Aible, Applied Scientific Intelligence, Eka Care, Foxconn, H Company, Palantir, Pyler, with Dell Technologies, Docusign, Infosys, K-Dense, Lila, Oracle, and Zefr evaluating (Source: NVIDIA Blog, April 28, 2026; wccftech, "NVIDIA Lines Up Foxconn, Palantir, and Oracle Behind Nemotron 3 Nano Omni…," April 2026). This is the first production-credible open multimodal release in the small-model class since DeepSeek V4 — and the first one explicitly architected for computer-use agents (NVIDIA cites a leap on the OSWorld benchmark for GUI navigation in preliminary evaluations).

Affinity MCP server — second commercial CRM-to-AI bridge of April. On April 28, 2026, Affinity announced the launch of its hosted Model Context Protocol server, connecting the Affinity CRM to ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Copilot via a single MCP endpoint (Source: Yahoo Finance, "Affinity Launches Model Context Protocol to Connect Relationship Intelligence to AI Tools for Private Capital," April 28, 2026; modelcontextprotocol.info, April 2026). The sector implication is straightforward: MCP has crossed from "experimental Anthropic protocol" to "default integration interface" for line-of-business SaaS — a transition aggregator coverage now describes as "de facto agent tool protocol" (Source: tokenmix.ai, "MCP Updates Changelog," 2026; sureprompts.com, "Model Context Protocol (MCP): The Complete 2026 Guide," 2026).

What This Means for You. For your AI implementation policies, do not modify your Microsoft-related policy language until tonight's print lands; queue the Thursday-morning update window. For team training, the Nemotron 3 Nano Omni release is a high-value teaching artifact — it is the cleanest current example of an open, GPU-friendly, multimodal, computer-use-capable model and pairs naturally with existing Claude Code lessons to illustrate "frontier-closed for orchestration, open-multimodal for perception" as a deployable pattern. For compliance posture, the Affinity MCP launch is the right small example to add to a Q2 compliance-curriculum module on third-party MCP-server risk — when a CRM's MCP server lives outside the buyer's perimeter and connects to multiple frontier labs simultaneously, the data-egress question is no longer hypothetical.

Divergence Analysis

Two divergences this cycle.

One — the open multimodal axis just opened a real second front. Until April 28, "open frontier" essentially meant "DeepSeek and a long tail." Nemotron 3 Nano Omni is the first production-credible open multimodal model that (a) is explicitly architected for computer-use agents, (b) ships day-one with a named adopter list dominated by enterprise infrastructure (Foxconn, Palantir, Oracle), and (c) runs on a single GPU at sub-prosumer hardware budgets. The closed-frontier narrative (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google) and the open-frontier narrative (DeepSeek, Nemotron) are now on different time-axes — the closed labs are quiet on capability this week, and the open multimodal axis just shipped a meaningful release. The implication is that the "vendor-neutral perception, vendor-anchored orchestration" architecture pattern should move from theoretical to recommended.

Two — Microsoft's earnings tonight will reset the entire market read on the April 27 restructure. The Tuesday SiliconAngle "trust anchor" framing of Google–Anthropic and the 24-hour MSFT rebound are both provisional until tonight's print. Watch for two specific lines on the call: the capacity-allocation language (whether Microsoft characterizes Azure third-party-supply tightness as easing or persisting) and the Copilot-stack language (whether Microsoft formally describes the M365 Copilot lineup as multi-model, with Anthropic Claude named, in the prepared remarks). Both lines will determine whether the Wednesday-morning client message is "the restructure is net-positive at the operating-leverage level" or "the capex commitment continues to outrun the revenue ramp."

What This Means for You. For your policies, two pending updates queue for Thursday morning: (a) post-earnings Microsoft talking points and (b) a Nemotron-3-Nano-Omni line item in the vendor-evaluation matrix as the recommended reference for "open, multimodal, on-device-capable, computer-use-ready."
