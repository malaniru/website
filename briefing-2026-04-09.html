1. AI Market Trends

Anthropic's revenue run rate crosses $30 billion. Anthropic disclosed that its annualized revenue run rate has surpassed $30 billion — more than triple the approximately $9 billion recorded at the end of 2025. The disclosure came alongside an expanded compute partnership with Google and Broadcom for multiple gigawatts of next-generation TPU capacity. More than 1,000 business customers now spend over $1 million annually on Claude services, a figure that has more than doubled since February 2026. The growth is being driven by enterprise deployments moving beyond pilots into production — software development, customer support, and internal data operations are the primary use cases. (Source: Bloomberg, "Anthropic Tops $30 Billion Run Rate, Seals Broadcom Deal," April 6, 2026; Anthropic, "Google-Broadcom Partnership Compute," April 2026; PYMNTS; The Register; Quartz)

However, this expansion comes amid regulatory friction. Anthropic is contesting a federal designation that labeled it a potential supply chain risk, and the company has warned the dispute could cost billions in lost revenue. More than 100 enterprise customers have reportedly raised concerns about continuing their relationships with Anthropic. The nature and scope of the federal designation require independent verification.

96% of enterprises now use AI agents; 94% report sprawl concerns. OutSystems published the results of a survey of nearly 1,900 global IT leaders (fielded December 2025–January 2026). Key findings: 96% of organizations are already using AI agents in some capacity, 97% are exploring system-wide agentic AI strategies, but 94% report concern that AI sprawl is increasing complexity, technical debt, and security risk. Only 12% have implemented a centralized platform to manage sprawl. Thirty-eight percent are mixing custom-built and pre-built agents, creating AI stacks that are difficult to standardize and secure. (Source: OutSystems / BusinessWire, April 7, 2026; SD Times; BetaNews)

Anthropic bans OpenClaw and third-party agents from flat-rate subscriptions. Effective April 4, Anthropic blocked Claude Pro and Max subscribers from routing their flat-rate plan usage through third-party agent frameworks like OpenClaw. Users who want to continue must pay under a new "extra usage" billing system. More than 135,000 OpenClaw instances were running at the time of the announcement; affected users face cost increases of up to 50x. OpenClaw creator Peter Steinberger — who joined OpenAI in February 2026 — called the move a lock-out of open source. (Source: TechCrunch, April 4, 2026; VentureBeat; The Register; TNW; Axios)

DigitalOcean acquires Katanemo Labs, joins S&P MidCap 400. DigitalOcean acquired Katanemo Labs (announced April 2–3), bringing its open-source agent platform Plano and agentic AI primitives into DigitalOcean's Inference Cloud. Katanemo CEO Salman Paracha joined as SVP of AI. Separately, DigitalOcean was selected to join the S&P MidCap 400 on April 6. (Source: DigitalOcean Investor Relations, April 2, 2026; S&P Global, April 6, 2026)

What This Means for You. The $30 billion run rate is the headline, but the underlying data point is more useful: over 1,000 customers spending $1M+ annually, doubled in two months. That's proof of production-grade enterprise adoption, not experimentation. The OutSystems data — 94% sprawl concern, only 12% with centralized governance — makes a direct case for putting an AI implementation policy in place before agent sprawl outpaces oversight. The OpenClaw ban reshapes pricing expectations for agentic AI workflows; organizations need to understand that flat-rate agent usage is not a given. The federal supply chain risk designation for Anthropic is worth monitoring — for any organization standardizing on a single AI provider, an escalation like this is a reminder to keep an approved fallback and a contingency plan.
