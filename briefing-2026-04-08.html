Executive Summary

Anthropic dropped the biggest product news of the week: Mythos, a restricted-access frontier model deployed through Project Glasswing for defensive cybersecurity, with 12 partners including Apple, Amazon, Microsoft, and CrowdStrike — and a claim of "thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities" discovered in its first weeks of operation. On the competitive front, Anthropic now holds 40% of enterprise LLM API spend per Menlo Ventures, overtaking OpenAI (27%) for the first time — while OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have begun sharing adversarial distillation intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum to counter Chinese model-copying operations. 1. AI Market Trends

Anthropic takes 40% of enterprise LLM API spend. Menlo Ventures' latest enterprise LLM market report confirms Anthropic now commands 40% of enterprise LLM API spend, up from 24% last year and 12% in 2023. OpenAI's share dropped to 27% from 50% in 2023. Google rose to 21% from 7% in 2023. Together, the three account for 88% of enterprise LLM API usage. Anthropic's code generation share is even more dominant at 42%, more than double OpenAI's 21%. (Source: Menlo Ventures, Enterprise LLM Market Report, via Yahoo Finance, April 2026)

Frontier labs unite against Chinese adversarial distillation. OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have begun sharing threat intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum to detect and counter adversarial distillation attempts by Chinese competitors. Anthropic documented 16 million exchanges from DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax — with DeepSeek deploying the most technically sophisticated methods, including using Claude to build censorship capabilities. This is modeled on cybersecurity-style threat intelligence sharing: when one lab detects an attack pattern, it flags it for the others. (Source: Bloomberg, April 6, 2026; Japan Times, April 7, 2026)

EY launches agentic AI across 130,000 audit professionals globally. EY announced the global rollout of enterprise-scale agentic AI in Assurance, embedding a multi-agent framework (built on Microsoft Azure, Foundry, and Fabric) into EY Canvas — the platform that processes 1.4 trillion lines of journal entry data per year. The rollout covers 130,000 professionals across 160,000 engagements in more than 150 countries. The stated goal: move from statistical sampling toward 100% population testing, with full end-to-end AI audit support by 2028. (Source: EY Newsroom, April 7, 2026; Bloomberg Tax; CPA Practice Advisor; Accountancy Age)

Enterprise AI adoption at 88%; agentic AI forecasted at 40% of business apps by year-end. Updated survey data puts regular enterprise AI use at 88%, up from 78% in the prior period. Separately, forecasts project 40% of business applications will employ AI agents by end of 2026, up from under 5% in 2025. Automation Anywhere reports AI agents now auto-resolve over 80% of IT support requests, cutting ITSM costs by up to 50%. The 40% forecast and Automation Anywhere figure are vendor-adjacent — treat as directional, not confirmed. (Source: TechCrunch; Automation Anywhere, via AI Agent Store, April 2026)

What This Means for You. The Anthropic market share data — 40% of enterprise LLM API spend, up from 24% a year ago — signals that the enterprise AI market is consolidating around a small set of frontier providers, which matters for how organizations plan vendor relationships. The EY announcement is a leading indicator for regulated sectors: if the Big Four are deploying agentic AI in audit across 130,000 professionals, boards in adjacent fields will face questions about their own AI governance posture within 12–18 months. The Frontier Model Forum anti-distillation effort reinforces a broader point: organizations need to understand not just how they use AI models, but how those models are protected upstream. Today's data gives organizations adopting AI fresh context for policy, training, and compliance conversations alike.

3. Agentic AI Advances and New Releases

Anthropic — Mythos model preview and Project Glasswing (April 7). Anthropic released a restricted preview of Mythos, described in a leaked internal memo as "one of its most powerful" models. The model is deployed exclusively through Project Glasswing, a defensive cybersecurity initiative with 12 partner organizations: Amazon, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, the Linux Foundation, Microsoft, and Palo Alto Networks among them. Forty organizations total have preview access. Mythos identified "thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities, many of them critical" — some one to two decades old — in both first-party and open-source software. Anthropic is limiting access specifically because the model's vulnerability-finding capability could be exploited by bad actors. No general availability planned; Anthropic says it will deploy Mythos-class models at scale "when new safeguards are in place." (Source: TechCrunch, April 7, 2026; CNBC; Fortune; CrowdStrike blog; Anthropic; SiliconANGLE; 9to5Mac; Simon Willison)

Anthropic — Claude Code source leak (March 31, covered April 7). An Anthropic employee inadvertently included a 59.8MB JavaScript source map file in version 2.1.88 of the @anthropic-ai/claude-code npm package. Within hours, the ~512,000-line TypeScript codebase was mirrored across GitHub and analyzed by thousands. Key revelations: KAIROS, an unreleased autonomous daemon mode; internal model codenames (Capybara = Claude 4.6 variant, Fennec = Opus 4.6, Numbat = unreleased model in testing); and "Undercover Mode," which prevents the AI from leaking internal identifiers. Security note: a separate supply-chain attack on the axios npm package occurred hours before the leak. (Source: VentureBeat; The Hacker News; DEV Community; NotebookCheck; The Register)

Claude outages (April 6–8). Claude experienced a major platform outage on April 7, with renewed connectivity issues on April 8. Users reported login failures, chat errors, and degraded performance. No post-incident review published yet. (Source: IBTimes Australia; TechRadar)

Microsoft — Agent Governance Toolkit (April 2). Microsoft released an open-source (MIT license) toolkit for runtime security governance of autonomous AI agents. First toolkit to address all 10 OWASP agentic AI risks with deterministic, sub-millisecond policy enforcement. Available for Python, Rust, TypeScript, Go, and .NET, with native hooks for LangChain, CrewAI, Google ADK, and Microsoft Agent Framework. Ships with 9,500+ tests and continuous fuzzing. Microsoft intends to move the project to a foundation for community governance. (Source: Microsoft Open Source Blog, April 2, 2026; InfoWorld; CSO Online; Help Net Security)

NousResearch — Hermes Agent v0.7.0 (April 3). NousResearch shipped "The Resilience Release" — an open-source self-improving agent with persistent memory, 40+ bundled skills, and multi-platform support (Telegram, Discord, Slack, WhatsApp, CLI). New in v0.7.0: pluggable memory providers, credential pool rotation, Camofox anti-detection browser, and deep security fixes across 168 PRs. (Source: NousResearch GitHub; AIToolly)

OpenAI — Codex enterprise expansion. OpenAI expanded ChatGPT Business and Enterprise with Codex-only seats on pay-as-you-go pricing, no rate limits, and token-based billing. Also lowered ChatGPT Business annual pricing. (Source: OpenAI; Releasebot)

Google — Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite. Google introduced Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite with 2.5x faster response times, priced at $0.25 per million input tokens. (Source: Google; LLM Stats)

Frontier Model Forum — Anti-distillation cooperation (April 6–7). The intelligence-sharing mechanism is modeled on cybersecurity ISACs, with pattern detection shared across labs in near-real-time. (Source: Bloomberg; Japan Times; Business Standard)

What This Means for You. Mythos is not a tool most organizations will deploy, but its result carries a clear message: frontier AI models are finding critical vulnerabilities — some one to two decades old — that human auditors missed for decades, which means AI governance is not optional. The Claude Code leak revealed KAIROS, an unreleased autonomous daemon mode; if Anthropic ships a persistent background agent, the way organizations build on Claude gains a new capability layer worth watching. The Agent Governance Toolkit is immediately actionable as a reference implementation for organizations drafting their own AI governance policies.
