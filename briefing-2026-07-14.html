1. AI Market Trends

Tuesday's tape is a continuation, not a reversal: the two threads that defined Monday — a Claude-stack product cadence and a memory-chip repricing — both advanced a step, and a third (agentic-AI venture funding) reasserted itself with fresh deals.

First, the Claude tooling cadence resumed. After a quiet weekend, Claude Code v2.1.208 shipped today, stacking on the v2.1.206–v2.1.207 reliability run. The headline additions are accessibility and enterprise-launch plumbing rather than model changes. The screen-reader mode and the CLAUDE_CODE_PROCESS_WRAPPER hook both point at the same trend the last month has made obvious: the frontier competition has moved from raw capability toward the operational surface — accessibility, corporate deployment, session durability — where agentic tools either survive contact with real IT environments or don't (Source: Claude Code changelog; Releasebot, July 14 — release-note aggregation). Separately, and worth flagging carefully: reporting indicates a promotional 50% increase in Claude Code weekly limits that had run since May 13 was scheduled to expire around July 14, with Anthropic expected to publish where permanent limits settle (Source: Releasebot, July 2026; press — ⚠️ reported and easily conflated with the separate Fable 5–linked rate-limit extension; confirm in-product before relying on either).

Second, the SK Hynix repricing entered day three. The record US debut — a ~13% first-day pop Friday, July 10 to a ~$168.01 close off $149 ADR pricing, a raise of roughly $26.5 billion billed as the largest-ever US first-time share sale by a foreign company — has now given back a meaningful slice of that gain. The US ADR (SKHY) tested the $150 area Tuesday, and Seoul-listed shares (000660.KS) fell about 5% more after Monday's record ~15.4% drop, leaving the ADR trading at a reported premium north of 20% to the home line (Reuters cited ~25.6% at one point) (Source: Bloomberg, July 14; FX Leaders, July 14; CNBC, July 13 — press; same-day prints not confirmed to a single authoritative feed — treat as reported). The framing has shifted from "morning after" to "how deep is the reset": several analysts characterize the pullback as mid-cycle profit-taking against still-tight AI-memory supply rather than a demand signal. Detail in Section 5.

Third, the broader semiconductor complex is digesting the same reset. Coverage continues to tie the early-July rout to a report that SK Hynix plans to slow high-bandwidth-memory (HBM) capacity expansion, with Micron the sharpest single-name hit — reported down about 13% in one session, roughly $138 billion of market value — and Intel and AMD off roughly 9% and 7%, while the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) shed about 5% after a record ~71% second quarter (Source: Intellectia.ai, "AI Chip Stocks Plunge," July 2026; Forbes, July 8 — press; figures vary by outlet and window). Nvidia has again traded apart from the group: reported holding just under $200, down about 17% from its May record but still up on the year, and outperforming the SMH even after a research note claimed it is a year behind on its next server-rack line (Source: CNBC, July 7; Yahoo Finance, July 2026 — press). Commentary also cites the Federal Reserve's more hawkish posture under Chair Kevin Warsh as an ambient pressure on high-multiple names, a macro attribution worth treating cautiously, not as a settled cause (Source: Intellectia.ai, July 2026 — press; ⚠️ macro causation is interpretive).

Fourth, agentic-AI venture funding kept concentrating on the operational and regulated-workflow layer. New rounds this month include Lyzr's ~$100 million Series B at a reported ~$500 million valuation — notable because the startup let its own agent, "SivaClaw," field questions from 130-plus investors and help run the raise; Taktile's $110 million Series C (led by Growth Equity at Goldman Sachs Alternatives) for an agentic decisioning platform aimed at banks and insurers; and Bespoke Labs' $40 million Series A (led by Wing VC) to build sandboxed environments where agents can be tested before production (Source: TechCrunch, July 9; Tech Startups, "VC Roundup," July 6; Crescendo.ai — press; private valuations unaudited). The throughline is unchanged: capital is flowing to agent execution infrastructure, enterprise control planes, and regulated vertical workflows: the governance-and-deployment layer, not just the models.

3. Agentic AI Advances & New Releases

Genuinely new or sharpened items since the July 13 briefing:

Claude Code v2.1.208 (today). The first release since v2.1.207 adds: an opt-in screen-reader mode (claude --ax-screen-reader, CLAUDE_AX_SCREEN_READER=1, or "axScreenReader": true) that renders plain text for assistive tech; a vimInsertModeRemaps setting to map two-key sequences (e.g., jj → Escape) in vim mode; a CLAUDE_CODE_PROCESS_WRAPPER environment hook for corporate launchers; mouse-click support for multi-select menus in fullscreen mode; automatic fast-mode restoration when switching back to a compatible model; and improved delivery of agent replies when sessions restart. It stacks on v2.1.207's auto-mode-by-default on Bedrock/Vertex/Foundry and the v2.1.206 reliability fixes (Source: Claude Code changelog; Releasebot, July 14 — release-note aggregation; confirm against the official changelog).

MCP 2026-07-28 — beta SDKs now available (sharpened). The largest revision since launch remains on track for a final spec July 28, and beta SDKs for Python, TypeScript, Go, and C# are now published against the release candidate. The headline change is a stateless protocol core — the Mcp-Session-Id header and protocol-level session are removed, so any request can land on any server instance and horizontal deployments no longer need sticky routing or shared session stores at the protocol layer. It also adds an Extensions framework, MCP Apps (server-rendered UIs), a Tasks extension for long-running work, OAuth 2.1/OIDC-aligned authorization, and a formal deprecation policy guaranteeing a minimum 12-month overlap between deprecation and removal (Source: Model Context Protocol blog, "The 2026-07-28 MCP Specification Release Candidate" and "Beta SDKs for the 2026-07-28 Spec" — primary).

SK Hynix — day-three reset (update). The ADR (SKHY) tested $150 Tuesday and Seoul shares fell about 5% more, extending Monday's record ~15% drop; analysts increasingly frame the move as a mid-cycle reset against still-tight AI-memory supply rather than a demand break (Source: Bloomberg, July 14; FX Leaders, July 14 — press). Detail in Section 5.

Gemini 3.5 Pro — still in preview (carry). GA remains a July target in limited Vertex AI enterprise preview; the loudest rumor is a July 17 date, with third-party reports citing a ground-up rebuild (after engineers reportedly found structural failures in recursive tool-calling and SVG generation), a rumored 2M-token context window, and a "Deep Think" reasoning layer. Google has published no confirmed GA date, model card, or pricing — treat the July 17 date and all spec claims as unconfirmed (Source: TechTimes, July 13; BigGo Finance; Geeky Gadgets — press/rumor; unconfirmed).

GPT-5.6 general availability (carry). Roughly five days into GA across ChatGPT, ChatGPT Work, Codex, and the API, with the three variants (Sol flagship, Terra mid-tier, Luna low-cost) live; ChatGPT Work is expanding from Pro/Enterprise/Edu toward Plus and Business plans (Source: Forbes, July 9; InfoWorld, July 2026 — press).

Fable 5 (carry). Included on paid plans (Pro, Max, Team, select Enterprise) through July 19 at up to 50% of weekly usage limits; prepaid usage credits ($10/M input, $50/M output — the highest pricing Anthropic has published for a GA model) begin July 20. Access past the window still requires enabling and funding credits in Settings → Usage (Source: Forbes, July 7; CybersecurityNews, July 2026 — press).

Carry items (scheduled or unchanged): ChatGPT Work — OpenAI's Cowork-style agentic app, live since July 9. Anthropic IPO — confidential S-1 filed June 1, targeting a fall listing (October reported). EU AI Act — Article 50 transparency obligations apply August 2, 2026 (verify specifics before external use).

What This Means for You. Two operational items and one to prepare for. Operationally: install Claude Code v2.1.208 if you run unattended automation (the session-restart and fast-mode fixes reduce silent failures), and note the screen-reader mode for accessibility needs. To prepare for: the MCP beta SDKs let you test the stateless-core change now, ahead of the July 28 final — worth confirming that any custom MCP server you maintain does not depend on protocol-level sessions, and that a 12-month deprecation window covers your migration. Everything else (Gemini 3.5 Pro, GPT-5.6, Fable 5's July 19 deadline) is a watch item, not an action for this morning.
