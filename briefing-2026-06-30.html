1. AI Market Trends

The story today is two deferrals dressed as decisions. The two nearest-dated carry items both resolved — and both resolved away from broad release. That, plus a genuine expansion of cyber-model access to critical infrastructure, is the substance of the day.

First — Gemini 3.5 Pro missed its June 30 window and is now a July item. The model announced at Google I/O on May 19 with a June GA target did not ship on June 30; reporting now places it as cleared and targeted for a July launch, still in limited preview for select Vertex AI enterprise customers (Source: TechTimes, June 29, 2026; cryptobriefing; Investing.com; Google declined to comment on the revised schedule). The analytically interesting part is why it is not gated: Gemini 3.5 Pro is described as the only major frontier model without a US government access restriction, with cybersecurity benchmark performance the apparent operative variable, Gemini 3.1 Pro reportedly scoring well below GPT-5.6 on Terminal-Bench 2.1 (Source: TechTimes, June 29, 2026; an inference attributed to reporting, not an official threshold). Treat the "July" date as a target, not a commitment. This model has now slipped at least once.

Second — the Mythos 5 allowlist expanded. This is the clearest change versus the prior day. Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick's June 26 letter authorized Mythos 5 release to roughly 100-plus US companies and federal agencies, including critical-infrastructure operators and the non-American employees at those organizations (Source: TechCrunch, CNN, The Hill, Cybersecurity Dive, June 26, 2026). Anthropic's June 29 update confirmed redeployment to US organizations that operate and defend critical infrastructure and said it continues working to expand access further (Source: Anthropic statement via explainx.ai; cybersecuritynews.com). The approved set grew, though it remains an administered allowlist, not a commercial release.

Third — Fable 5 is still dark on Day 18. Anthropic's broader general-purpose frontier model remains offline for consumers, API, Claude Code, and international users, suspended since June 12, with no announced return date; negotiations continue and some outlets report restoration "in coming days," but the Pentagon/NSA sign-off remains the gating step (Source: explainx.ai; Totalum; Axios, June 27). "Coming days" is reporting, not a commitment.

What This Means for You

Today reinforces the core lesson: govern for the state that exists, not the one the headlines imply is imminent. Two reportedly-near releases (Gemini 3.5 Pro, Fable 5) again did not arrive on their watched dates, while access to the most sensitive model (Mythos 5) is being administered case-by-case to a named, expanding list of critical-infrastructure operators — a concrete example of AI access functioning like an export-controlled good. A defensible AI policy plans around generally-available, governable tooling and treats access-gated frontier and cyber models as out of scope until they ship with published terms. For teams in production, the practical guidance is unchanged: keep work on stable, governable business-tier models, none of which are touched by the Mythos and Fable controls or the Gemini delay.
