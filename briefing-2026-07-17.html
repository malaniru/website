1. AI Market Trends

Thursday inverted Wednesday's setup. A day earlier the market had discriminated within the AI complex — selling memory chips while rotating into big-cap tech. On July 16 there was nowhere to rotate: the two largest AI stories of the day were both negative, and they compounded.

First, the Gemini delay. Bloomberg reported that Google is months behind on Gemini 3.5 Pro, its intended flagship, because the model underperformed internal targets — especially on coding — at a moment when Anthropic and OpenAI have shipped models that outpace Google's current offerings on software generation (Source: Bloomberg, July 16; FourWeekMBA, "Gemini 3.5 Pro Is Delayed, and the Coding Gap Is Google's Real Problem," July 16 — press). The market treated this as a capability-credibility event, not a scheduling footnote: Alphabet closed down about 4.4% and shed roughly $200 billion in market value in a single session (Source: CNBC; Yahoo Finance, "Alphabet stock falls 4% on report of Gemini AI model delays," July 16 — press; corroborated). The rumored July 17 GA — which prior briefings flagged repeatedly as unconfirmed — will not happen, and Google has still published no confirmed date, model card, or pricing.

Second, the semiconductor selloff broadened on a counterintuitive TSMC read. TSMC posted a better-than-expected second quarter and reiterated confidence in more than 30% full-year 2026 revenue growth in US-dollar terms, but it raised its 2026 capital-expenditure outlook to $60-64 billion, above the prior $52-56 billion range, to expand advanced-node and CoWoS packaging capacity (Source: TradingKey, "TSMC Q2 earnings," July 15-16; TipRanks, "TSMC Will Report Q2 Earnings on July 16," July 2026 — press; specific per-ADR EPS/revenue figures were pre-report estimates, treat exact actuals as reported, not confirmed here). The tape read the heavier spend as margin and AI-capex risk rather than a bullish demand tell — the SMH ETF fell about 4%, Arm dropped more than 5%, and the chip weakness spilled into Asia overnight (Source: Yahoo Finance; CNBC, July 16 — press).

Third, memory slid into a bear market. The CXMT overhang from Wednesday did not clear; it deepened. Micron's market capitalization fell below $1 trillion as the stock slid on the session, and SK Hynix, SanDisk, and Western Digital each fell about 7% while the Roundhill Memory ETF (DRAM) dropped 7%; SK Hynix's Seoul-listed line tumbled more than 11% (Source: 24/7 Wall St., "SK Hynix and SanDisk Sink 7%, Micron Falls 5% as China's CXMT Readies an $8.6B Memory IPO," July 16; BigGo Finance, "Memory Chip Stocks Bloodbath: Micron's Market Cap Drops Below $1 Trillion," July 16; TradingKey, July 16 — press; same-day single-name percentages vary by feed). CXMT's roughly $8.5-8.6 billion Shanghai STAR Market IPO, still set to list July 27, remains the structural fear: a well-funded Chinese DRAM challenger accelerating a supply glut. The honest read is unchanged from yesterday — memory pricing power is now the contested variable, and this week the sign has stayed negative.

Fourth, agentic-AI venture funding stayed heavy and continued to reward vertical execution. July's agent-startup total is tracked near $1.8 billion across 12-plus deals, with more than 60% of rounds at Series B or later and average check sizes near $150 million on real revenue traction. This week's fresh entry: India's Emergent became a unicorn with a $130 million Series C at a roughly $1.5 billion post-money valuation, barely a year after launch — an AI coding platform, joining a July board led by Harvey AI ($200M Series C, ~$2.1B), Lovable ($200M Series B, ~$2.8B), Glean ($180M Series D, ~$2.7B), Hebbia ($130M Series B, ~$1.0B), and Norm AI ($120M Series C for compliance agents) (Source: TechCrunch, "Indian AI coding startup Emergent becomes a unicorn," July 15; gravity.fast, "AI Agent Funding Tracker: Q3 2026"; aifunding.me, July 2026 — press; private valuations unaudited, re-confirm before external use). The throughline holds: capital concentrates in agent execution infrastructure, developer platforms, and regulated vertical workflows.

GPT-5.6 general availability (carry, sharpened). About a week into GA across ChatGPT, ChatGPT Work, Codex, and the API. Three variants — Sol (flagship, with an "ultra" mode that delegates to submodels and a new "Max" reasoning-effort setting), Terra (mid-tier, targeting GPT-5.5-level quality at roughly half the cost), and Luna (low-cost, fast). OpenAI reports Codex has passed 8 million users in days; Sam Altman publicly flagged "hiccups" as demand outran capacity. ChatGPT Work — OpenAI's Cowork-style agentic app — continues rolling out across tiers (Source: Axios, "OpenAI releases GPT-5.6," July 9; The New Stack, "OpenAI hits 8 million Codex users," July 2026 — press; efficiency/user figures vendor-reported).

MCP 2026-07-28 — release candidate, beta SDKs available (carry). The largest revision since launch is final on July 28 (now ~11 days out). The headline change is a stateless protocol core — protocol-level sessions and the initialize/initialized handshake are removed, and the Streamable HTTP transport now routes on Mcp-Method/Mcp-Name headers so gateways can run behind a plain load balancer without inspecting the body. List/resource results carry cache TTLs; an Extensions framework, Tasks, and MCP Apps (server-rendered UIs) round out the change; auth aligns with OAuth 2.1. A new lifecycle policy guarantees at least twelve months between deprecation and removal. Beta SDKs for Python, TypeScript, Go, and C# are published against the RC (Source: Model Context Protocol blog, "The 2026-07-28 MCP Specification Release Candidate" and "Beta SDKs for the 2026-07-28 Spec" — primary).

Carry items (scheduled or unchanged): Fable 5 — included on paid plans through July 19 at up to 50% of weekly usage limits; prepaid usage credits ($10/M input, $50/M output) begin July 20. ChatGPT Work — OpenAI's Cowork-style agentic app, live since July 9. Anthropic IPO — confidential S-1 filed June 1; reported investor meetings July 15 ahead of a potential fall listing under ticker ANTH; last-round valuation figures vary across reports (a ~$965B figure has circulated on the most recent Series H) — treat as reported, not verified.
