OutSystems: 94% of enterprises concerned about agentic AI sprawl. OutSystems published its 2026 State of AI Development report on April 13, surveying nearly 1,900 global IT leaders. Key findings: 96% of organizations are already using AI agents, 97% are exploring system-wide agentic AI strategies, and 49% describe their capabilities as advanced or expert. But 94% report that AI sprawl is increasing complexity, technical debt, and security risk. Only 12% have implemented a centralized platform to manage it. 38% are mixing custom-built and pre-built agents, creating stacks that are difficult to standardize and secure. (Source: OutSystems, April 13, 2026; PR Newswire, April 2026)

What This Means for You. The OutSystems report is the week's most directly actionable finding. "94% concerned about sprawl, only 12% have centralized governance" is precisely the gap an AI implementation policy is meant to close. For organizations where AI adoption is accelerating but governance is lagging, the data underscores the urgency of putting that policy in place. The finding that 38% are mixing custom-built and pre-built agents is a useful prompt for training: if staff are assembling agent stacks without standardization, hands-on training is not optional. And the sprawl problem is also a data-handling problem — ungoverned agents interacting with sensitive records and evaluations create compliance exposure that grows with every unmanaged deployment.
